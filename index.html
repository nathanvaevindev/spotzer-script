Before You Call
Context & Preparation
These are not cold leads. Know the situation before you pick up the phone.
🔁 Who you're calling
  • Long-standing customers who had a website built and managed through Telenet
  • Spotzer Digital built and maintained their site — we already have a relationship with them
  • They've received an email about the transition to Online Essentials
  • They're not a new prospect to convince — they're an existing customer to retain and grow
💡 Your mindset on this call
You're not selling to a stranger. You're the new account manager for someone who already trusted this company. Warm, peer-to-peer, confident.
PrepBefore each call, know:
1. Do they still have an active website with us?
If yes — when was it last updated? Is it mobile-friendly?

2. What product were they on through Telenet?
Basic site only? Any extras? This tells you what upsell gap exists.

3. Have they opened the transition email?
If yes — they know what's coming. If no — they may be surprised.

4. What's their business type?
Knowing their sector lets you speak their language immediately.
Phase 01
Introduction
Lead with the opportunity. Short, sharp, compelling — then context.
YouOpening line
"Hi [Name], my name is Nathan from Online Essentials. I'm calling because Telenet has given all their current customers the opportunity to upgrade their business online and see how your business has been doing so far. Is now a good time to talk for a few minutes?"
💡 Why this order works: Lead with what's in it for them — an opportunity, not an explanation. The context comes after they say yes.
YouAfter they say yes — give the context
"Great. And as you're likely aware, a bit of background — we're the team that built and managed your website through Telenet. Which means that we'll now directly work with you under Online Essentials, so we're able to give more — covering everything you need to grow your business online.

As I mentioned my name is Nathan. I'm your new point of contact — and my goal today is simple: understand how your business is doing and make sure we're actually helping you grow."
Phase 02
Reconnect
Acknowledge the existing relationship before asking anything. Make them feel known.
YouAcknowledge the history
"[Name], you've been with us for a while now [years], I hope the business has been growing in these years. First what I'd love to do today is just take a few minutes to understand how the website has been working for you, whether anything has changed in the business, and see if there's anything we can be doing better."

"This isn't a hard sell — I just want to make sure we're actually delivering value for you."
💡 Why this works: Long-term customers often feel ignored once they've signed up. "I don't take that for granted" immediately differentiates you from every vendor who just takes renewal money and disappears.
YouQuick check-in on their site
"[Name], have you had a chance to look at [website name] recently? How are you feeling about it — does it still represent where your business is today?"

[Let them talk. Most will either say "it's fine" (dig deeper) or mention something that bothers them — that's your opening.]
Phase 03
Discovery
They already have a website. Go deeper — performance, visibility, growth. Click any question to select it.
💡 Shift in focus: With these customers, ask "is your website actually working for you" — not "do you have one."
Q1
Website performance
"Your website is often the first impression someone gets of your business — does it represent the quality of what you actually do?"
"When did you last update your website? Does it still reflect where your business is today?"
"Have you checked lately how your website looks compared to your competitors? Are they showing up stronger than you online?"
"Are you getting enquiries through your website, or are most of your leads still coming through word of mouth?"
Opens website performance conversation → Website upgrade
Q2
Google visibility
"If someone in [city] searches for [their service] right now — do you think you'd come up?"
"Do you know where you rank on Google for your main service?"
"Would you be willing to invest in being found organically on Google over the next 6–12 months?"
"Is ranking higher on Google something you'd prioritise as a growth strategy?"
"Are you aware that SEO is a long-term investment — and does that appeal to you?"
Visibility gap → Listings / If SEO intent confirmed → SEO / Local Search
Q3
Business changes
"Has anything changed in the business since we last worked on your site — new services, different focus, new location?"
"Have you launched any new services or products that aren't on your site yet?"
"Does your website still feel like you — or has your business moved on from it?"
Reveals outdated content → Website update / upgrade
Q4
Reviews & reputation
"What does your Google rating look like right now — are customers leaving reviews?"
"Do you respond to your reviews — good and bad?"
Opens reputation management → Listings Standard / Ultimate
Q5
Customer acquisition
"Outside of word of mouth — how are new customers finding you today?"
"Is your online presence actively bringing you new business, or is it more passive?"
"If you could get more leads online without relying on referrals — would that make a difference?"
Opens growth conversation → SEO / Advertising
Q6
Social media
"Are you active on social media at all — Instagram, Facebook? How consistent has that been?"
"Do you feel like your social media reflects the quality of your business?"
"How much time do you actually spend on social media for your business each week?"
"Is staying consistent on social something you struggle with?"
"If your social media was always active and on-brand without you thinking about it — would that help?"
Opens social visibility conversation → AI Social Visibility
Q7
Paid advertising
"Have you ever tried Google Ads or paid advertising? How did that go?"
"Are you currently spending anything on online advertising?"
"Have you ever felt like you're invisible on Google compared to competitors?"
"Is getting more leads quickly something that's important to you right now?"
"If every €1 you spent on ads brought back €3 — would you want to know how?"
Opens paid advertising → Online Advertising
Q8
Goals
"What's your main focus for the business over the next 12 months — grow, stabilise, expand?"
"If everything went well this year — what would that look like for your business?"
"Where do you want to be in a year from now, and is your online presence set up to get you there?"
"Are you where you want to be in terms of growth, or is there more potential you're not reaching?"
"Is your business growing the way you want it to — or do you feel like you're leaving customers on the table?"
Anchors recommendations to their actual ambition
TipReading the signals
As they answer, mentally tag what you're hearing:

Site feels outdated → Website ↗ Can't be found → SEO ↗ Low reviews → Listings ↗ Needs leads fast → Ads ↗ Inconsistent social → AI Social ↗
Phase 04
Diagnosis
Reflect back what you heard. Name the gap. Make them feel understood before you offer anything.
📋 Questions that resonated this call
No questions selected yet — go back to Discovery and click the ones that landed.
YouSummarise before pitching
"Okay, so from what you've told me — [paraphrase 2–3 specific things]. What I'm hearing is that [core problem or gap]. That's something we can help with directly through Online Essentials — let me show you exactly how."
💡 The website lens check — use this if they seem satisfied but the site is dated:

"When was the last time you looked at your site through the eyes of a new customer? Not as someone who knows the business — but as a complete stranger deciding whether to call you or a competitor?"
TipRed flags — use your health check
Run the health check on their website before or during the call and reference what you see specifically:

"I've actually had a look at your website — and based on what I can see, [specific finding: not mobile-friendly / slow loading / missing contact form / low Google ranking]. That's exactly the kind of thing we can fix."

The more specific you are, the more real it becomes. Don't speak in generalities — name what you see.
Phase 05
Presentation
Frame Online Essentials as the natural evolution — not a new product, a better version of what they already have.
YouTransition into product(s)
"So what we've built with Online Essentials is essentially everything we were doing for you through Telenet — but now as a complete package you work with us on directly, with more features, better support, and the ability to grow it as your business grows.

Based on what you've told me, I'd focus on [1–2 specific things]. Let me walk you through those."
"We're not just building a site — we're building trust and visibility for your business."
"From domain to design, SEO to support — everything in one place, one team, one bill."
"You've already trusted us with your website. This is just the full picture of what we can do."
Phase 06
Packages
Lead with a package — it's simpler for the customer and better for value. Open only what fits.
💡 Package first: Based on what you heard in Discovery, recommend the package that best matches their situation. Only go to individual products if they need something specific a package doesn't cover.

Phase 08
Objections
These customers already know us. Objections here are mostly about value, price, or change aversion — not trust.
TransitionObjections about switching from Telenet
ObjectionResponse
"I'm happy with what I had through Telenet"That makes sense — because we're the ones who built and managed it. Nothing changes in terms of quality. What does change is you now work with us directly — faster support, more flexibility, and a full suite of products we couldn't offer through that channel.
"Why can't I just stay on Telenet?"The Telenet partnership has come to an end — that's why we're making this transition. But you're not losing anything. You're actually gaining a more direct relationship with the team that's been doing the work all along.
"I didn't ask for this change"I completely understand — and I appreciate your patience with it. I'm personally here to make sure this transition goes smoothly for you.
PriceHandling cost objections
ObjectionResponse
"It's more expensive than before"You're getting more than before — a more capable platform, more products, and direct access to our team. But let's be practical: if this brings you even one extra customer a month, what does that cover? For most businesses, it more than pays for itself.
"I can do it myself"Absolutely — tools like Wix exist. But you'd be trading our expertise and support for something you manage yourself. If that's 5–10 hours a month, what does that cost you? And if something breaks, who fixes it?
"I need to think about it"Of course. Can I ask — is it the price, the product, or something else? I'd rather understand and come back with the right answer than leave you with an open question.
"I already have someone doing this"Fair enough — when did you last review the results they're delivering? We're happy to do a quick audit and show you exactly where you stand. If they're doing a great job, brilliant. If not, you'll know.

✅ If they say yes

Confirm the details, then say:

"Perfect. What we'll do now is get you booked in for an onboarding call — that's where we'll walk you through everything, get all the details we need from you, and make sure the setup is exactly right for your business. You'll hear from us shortly to confirm the time."

Confirm their email address, keep the energy warm. They've been a customer — treat them like one.

⏳ If they need time

"No problem — can I send you a summary of what we discussed? And when's a good time to follow up — would Thursday or Friday work?" Always book the follow-up before ending the call.

🚫 If they say no

"I appreciate your honesty. Can I ask — is it the product, the price, or the timing? I'd rather understand now so I can come back when the fit is right."

📋 Always

Log the outcome, note objections, set a follow-up reminder. A no today from a long-term customer is often a yes in 3 months.

"You've trusted us with your website for years. This is just the next step in that same relationship."
"We're not asking you to take a leap of faith — you already know the quality of our work."
